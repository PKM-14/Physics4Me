1. Intuition — What Magnetism Actually Does

— What Magnetism Actually Does

Magnetism is fundamentally different from electric forces. Electric fields act on charges simply for existing. Magnetic fields only act when charges are moving.

A useful analogy is running through rain. If you stand still, rain falls straight down. But when you move, the rain appears to hit you at an angle. Motion changes how you experience the same environment — magnetism works in a similar way.

No motion → no magnetic force.

2. Lorentz Force — The Core Law

— The Core Law

Observed Behaviour

A charged particle moving through a magnetic field is deflected sideways. Its speed does not change, only its direction.

Why This Must Be True

If the force acted along motion, the particle would speed up or slow down. Since this does not happen, the force must be perpendicular to velocity.

F = qvB sin(θ)
  • q → charge
  • v → velocity
  • B → magnetic field
  • sin(θ) → selects perpendicular component
Magnetism is geometry — only sideways motion interacts.

Deep Insight

Magnetic forces cannot do work because they are always perpendicular to motion. This is why they bend paths instead of changing speed.

3. Circular Motion — Derived, Not Memorised

— Derived, Not Memorised

If velocity is fully perpendicular to the magnetic field, the force is always sideways, creating circular motion.

qvB = mv² / r
r = mv / qB
  • Higher velocity → larger radius
  • Stronger field → tighter curve

Extension Insight

If velocity is not fully perpendicular, the particle moves in a helix (spiral), combining circular motion and forward motion.

4. Fleming’s Left Hand Rule — Direction

Magnetic forces exist in three perpendicular directions: field, current, and force. This reflects the cross-product nature of the Lorentz force.

Magnetism is inherently 3D — it cannot be understood in straight-line thinking.

5. Motor Effect — Electricity to Motion

— Electricity to Motion

From First Principles

A current is moving charges. Each charge experiences the Lorentz force, so the entire wire experiences a force.

F = BIL
  • B → field strength
  • I → current
  • L → length in field
More current = more moving charges = larger force.

Motor Mechanism

Motors work by flipping current direction so forces keep pushing in the same rotational direction.

Worked Insight

Why does increasing current make a motor stronger? Because more charge per second flows, increasing total Lorentz force.

6. Generator Effect — Motion to Electricity

— Motion to Electricity

Reverse Thinking

Moving a conductor through a magnetic field forces its charges to move, generating current.

Motion + field → charge movement → current

Energy Reality

Electrical energy comes from mechanical work. You must push the generator — it does not produce energy freely.

Worked Insight

Why does a generator become harder to turn when connected to a circuit? Because induced currents create opposing forces (Lenz’s Law), requiring more input energy.

7. Electromagnetic Induction (Faraday’s Law)

(Faraday’s Law)
ε = - dΦ / dt
  • Φ → magnetic flux
  • dΦ/dt → rate of change
No change in magnetic flux → no induced voltage.

Key Idea

It is not the field itself, but the change in the field that creates voltage.

8. Lenz’s Law — Conservation of Energy

The induced current always opposes the change that created it.

Nature resists change to preserve energy balance.

Without this, generators could produce infinite energy — which is impossible.

9. Common Misconceptions

  • Magnetic fields do work → false (force is perpendicular)
  • Current directly causes force → incomplete (moving charges do)
  • Stronger magnets always increase speed → false (they curve motion)

10. Unification — The Bigger Picture

Electricity and magnetism are two aspects of the same phenomenon: electromagnetism.

Magnetism arises because motion changes how electric fields are observed — a consequence of relativity.

Electric fields + motion = magnetic effects
